Lewis Latimer

Engineer, Inventor, Poet

Lewis Latimer (1848-1928) was an African American inventor, draftsman, and engineer whose contributions significantly impacted the development of the incandescent light bulb. Despite facing the challenges of a life born into slavery, Latimer's hard work, intelligence, and creativity propelled him to become a prominent figure in the field of invention.

A time line of Lewis Latimer's life:

  • 1848 - Born in Chelsea, Massachusetts
  • 1864 - Joined the U.S. Navy at the age of 16 and served as a Landsman on the U.S.S. Massasoit
  • 1865 - After being honorably discharged from the Navy, he began working
  • Late 1860's - He gains employment as an office assistant at a patent law firm, where he develops his drafting skills.
  • 1874 - Latimer was hired as a draftsman by Hiram Maxim, the inventor of the Maxim gun.
  • 1876 - Latimer was hired by Alexander Graham Bell as a draftsman and an expert witness in patent litigation on the telephone.
  • 1881 - Latimer patented a method of making carbon filaments for the Maxim electric incandescent lamp.
  • 1882 - Latimer patented an improved process for manufacturing carbon filaments for light bulbs.
  • 1884 - He becomes a founding member of the Edison Pioneers, a group of engineers who worked with Thomas Edison.
  • 1918 - He becomes one of the first African American members of the prestigious Edison Pioneers organization.
  • 1928 - Lewis Latimer dies at the age of 80, leaving behind a remarkable legacy of innovation and achievement.
